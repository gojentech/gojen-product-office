## Purpose

Define shared product management terminology used across discovery, requirements, roadmap, and release documentation.

## Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **PRD** | Product Requirements Document — product-facing specification of what to build. |
| **BRD** | Business Requirements Document — business needs and constraints that drive the PRD. |
| **Discovery** | Structured investigation to frame problems and opportunities before build commitment. |
| **Persona** | Archetype representing a class of users or buyers. |
| **User story** | Short narrative of user value with acceptance criteria. |
| **Acceptance criteria** | Testable conditions that determine story or requirement completion. |
| **Roadmap** | Sequenced view of themes and initiatives over a planning horizon. |
| **Backlog** | Ordered list of candidate work; not a commitment by itself. |
| **Sprint** | Timeboxed delivery interval used for planning and review. |
| **MVP** | Minimum Viable Product — smallest release that validates a learning or value hypothesis. |
| **Release** | Packaged set of changes made available to users or systems. |
| **Decision log** | Chronological store of durable product or portfolio decisions. |
| **Risk register** | Tracked inventory of risks with owners and responses. |
| **Traceability** | Ability to follow a requirement from business need through design, build, and release. |
| **Scope** | Explicit boundary of what is included and excluded. |
| **Assumption** | Believed-true statement that, if false, may invalidate plans. |
| **Dependency** | External or internal prerequisite required for progress. |
| **Stakeholder** | Person or group with interest or influence over outcomes. |
