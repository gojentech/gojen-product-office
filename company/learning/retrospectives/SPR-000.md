## Objectives

1. Establish GAIOS v1.0 as the company operating system in GitHub.
2. Deliver navigable governance, memory, and operating documentation without modifying existing standards.
3. Enable Founder Board approval of GAIOS adoption.
4. Prepare the repository for the first product discovery sprint.

---

## Achievements

- GAIOS entry points, AI governance, memory, and operating domains delivered.
- Existing GPO standards preserved (additive-only foundation).
- Founder Board approved GAIOS as the official OS ([DEC-001](../../decision-register/DEC-001-GAIOS-Adoption.md) / [DEC-GPO-002](../../decision-register/dec-gpo-002-gaios-v1-adoption.md)).
- Foundation sprint archived as permanent SSOT history ([SPR-000-GAIOS-Foundation.md](../../sprints/SPR-000-GAIOS-Foundation.md)).
- Transition opened to SubscriptionOS Discovery ([CURRENT-SPRINT.md](../../ai-governance/CURRENT-SPRINT.md)).

---

## Challenges

- Dual use of CURRENT-SPRINT as both live status and history risked overwriting the audit trail.
- Large parallel corpus required a post-assembly index pass for complete discoverability.
- Root README/INDEX still do not surface GAIOS (intentionally untouched during foundation).

---

## Lessons learned

- Keep CURRENT-SPRINT aligned to the active sprint; archive completed sprints under `company/sprints/`.
- Prefer permanent retrospective records under `company/learning/retrospectives/`.
- Cite Board decisions with stable register IDs; use DEC-001 as the board citation alias for GAIOS adoption.
- Product discovery work belongs in product backlog paths, not only company doctrine folders.

---

## Process improvements

1. Close every sprint with archive + retrospective + action register updates before opening the next.
2. Track product stories under `products/<product>/backlog/`.
3. Refresh roadmaps at sprint boundaries so milestones match Board decisions.
4. Use Founder Board action registers for cross-owner commitments.
