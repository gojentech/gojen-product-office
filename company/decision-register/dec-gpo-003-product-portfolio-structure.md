| **Options Considered** | See below |
| **Decision** | Maintain two **active** products — **Subscription OS** and **Pawn Management** — each with its own product workspace, research threads, and decision/risk scope, under one company GAIOS operating system. |
| **Reason** | Separates domain complexity (SaaS billing vs pawn/collateral ops) while sharing company standards, governance, and AI operating rules. |

## Options Considered

| Option | Summary | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A — Dual active portfolio | SOS + PAW both active | Diversified bets; shared OS | Focus risk (see Risk GPO-003) |
| B — Single product only | Kill or freeze one | Maximum focus | Abandons second market learning |
| C — Single platform brand | One product, two modules | Unified GTM | Misleading; domains differ sharply |
| D — Agency / custom only | No productized portfolio | Cash optionality | No scalable SaaS asset |

## Decision

Adopt **Option A**. Portfolio structure:

| Slot | Product | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Active | Subscription OS (SOS) | Active — lifecycle workspace established |
| Active | Pawn Management (PAW) | Active — workspace established; lifecycle expansion by approval |
| Future | Future products | Placeholder under GAIOS products map when created |
| Archived | Archived products | For retired offerings only |

## Reason

- Domains do not share a natural single catalog or compliance model.
- Company-level GAIOS absorbs shared process cost so products can stay focused.
- Explicit dual-active status forces prioritization rituals (see dual-product risk mitigation).

## Impact

| Area | Impact |
| --- | --- |
| Research | Dual tracks in Research Center with shared frameworks |
| Roadmaps | Separate product roadmaps; company roadmap shows sequencing |
| Engineering | Prefer separate codebases unless a shared-service decision is Approved |
| Sales/Marketing | Distinct ICPs and messaging; no forced umbrella narrative |
