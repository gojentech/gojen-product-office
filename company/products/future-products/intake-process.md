## Purpose

Define how a product idea becomes a formal candidate in the Gojen Technology portfolio pipeline.

## Intake Packet Requirements

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Working title | Clear, non-marketing name |
| Problem statement | Who hurts, how often, and why now |
| Customer hypothesis | Segment and buyer |
| Strategic fit | Why Gojen; relation to Subscription OS / Pawn Management |
| Rough solution shape | One paragraph, not a full PRD |
| Capacity ask | Estimated founder/Product Office attention |
| Risks | Top three risks |
| Recommendation | Advance / Hold / Reject suggestion |

## Process Steps

1. Author drafts intake packet using this process.
2. Product Office checks completeness against the packet table.
3. Evaluation uses [evaluation-criteria.md](./evaluation-criteria.md).
4. Founder Board decides Advance, Hold, or Reject.
5. Outcome is recorded under Future Products (or moved to archive/active).

## Advance Outcomes

When Advance is approved:

1. Allocate scope code (three letters).
2. Create root lifecycle workspace under `products/<name>/`.
3. Create GAIOS operating summary folder under `company/products/<name>/`.
4. Assign Product Owner accountability.

## Hold / Reject Outcomes

| Outcome | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Hold | Remain in Future Products with review date |
| Reject | Move rationale to archived-products with archive policy |

## SLA Guidance

| Step | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Completeness check | Within 5 business days of submission |
| Founder decision | Within one Founder Board cycle after complete packet |
