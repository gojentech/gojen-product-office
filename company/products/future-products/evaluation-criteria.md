## Purpose

Provide a consistent scoring model so product candidates are judged on strategy, evidence, and capacity — not enthusiasm alone.

## Scoring Model (1–5)

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strategic fit | Weak or conflicting | Adjacent | Core to Gojen direction |
| Problem severity | Mild inconvenience | Recurring operational pain | Mission-critical pain |
| Market access | Unclear path | Plausible channel | Founder-accessible beachhead |
| Differentiation | Commodity | Partial edge | Clear durable edge |
| Capacity realism | Crowds out active products | Stretch but feasible | Fits current capacity plan |
| Risk posture | Unowned high risks | Manageable with plan | Risks understood and owned |

## Decision Thresholds

| Aggregate signal | Typical decision |
| --- | --- |
| Strong across fit + capacity | Advance |
| Strong fit, weak capacity | Hold with revisit date |
| Weak fit or severe risk gaps | Reject or reframe |
| Conflicts with SOS/PAW priority | Hold unless Founder Board re-prioritizes |

## Evaluation Record

Each evaluation must capture:

1. Scores by criterion
2. Assumptions
3. Capacity impact on Subscription OS and Pawn Management
4. Recommendation and decision
5. Owner and date

## Anti-Patterns

- Approving ideas without an intake packet
- Scoring only market size while ignoring capacity
- Creating root product workspaces before Advance approval
