## Description

Early-stage teams naturally capture truth in chats, local notes, and AI threads. Without promotion discipline, founders make decisions from incomplete or conflicting copies of reality. Dual products amplify the risk because Subscription OS and Pawn Management contexts collide in the same founder calendars.

## Triggers

- Material decision left only in a chat transcript
- Research conclusion not written into Research Center
- Conflicting “latest” versions in email vs repository

## Consequences

- Rework and contradictory AI answers
- Slow onboarding of future hires
- Loss of audit trail for governance

## Mitigation

| Control | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| SSOT policy | [DEC-GPO-001](../decision-register/dec-gpo-001-github-as-ssot.md) |
| GAIOS structure | Registers, research, wiki, templates |
| Promotion rule | Durable claims must land in GitHub within the same working week |
| Session template | [AI Session Template](../templates/ai-session-template.md) requires “docs to update” |
| Weekly founder log | Capture open loops and force promotion |

## Residual Risk

Medium — human discipline remains the weak link; tooling reduces but does not eliminate fragmentation.
