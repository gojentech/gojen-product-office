## Description

DEC-GPO-003 correctly separates domains, but founder time is finite. Without explicit sequencing, both products may progress slowly, research stays shallow, and neither reaches a credible beachhead.

## Triggers

- Same sprint claims major milestones for both products without capacity model
- Shared engineering started before product-scoped spikes complete
- Founder calendar shows constant context switching without weekly priority

## Consequences

- Missed learning velocity per market
- Demoralizing half-built surfaces
- Forced emergency freeze under time pressure

## Mitigation

| Control | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Explicit dual-active portfolio | [DEC-GPO-003](../decision-register/dec-gpo-003-product-portfolio-structure.md) |
| Weekly priority rule | Each week names a **primary** product for deep work |
| Separate research tracks | Research Center dual sections |
| Capacity checkpoint | Quarterly review; freeze option if lag exceeds threshold |
| Dashboard transparency | Blocked items and focus product visible weekly |

## Freeze Criteria (examples)

- Two consecutive months with no validated customer interviews on the secondary product
- Critical compliance blocker unresolved while both roadmaps expand
- Founder Board vote that one product enters **Paused** status

## Residual Risk

Medium — inherent to dual-product startups; managed, not eliminated.
